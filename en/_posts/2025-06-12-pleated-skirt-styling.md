## Why the Pleated Skirt Never Gets Old

The charm of the pleated skirt is that it is "crisp when still, flying when moving." Its pleats open and close as you walk, turning the play of light across the cloth into visual motion. It offers freedom of movement through its volume while flattering the figure through its lines — which is exactly why it endures.

### Three Summer Scenarios

**Commute**
- Finely pressed midi skirt + minimal knit vest + loafers
- Crisp pleats and a restrained top create a sharp yet soft workwear mood.

**Weekend Casual**
- Accordion-pleat skirt + white tee + flats
- Let the movement of the skirt lead; the simpler the top, the better.

**Evening Gathering**
- Sheeny pleated skirt + slip top + heeled sandals
- The flow of pleats under light naturally lends a touch of occasion.

### The Key to Balance: Simple Top, Rich Bottom

A pleated skirt already carries plenty of visual information, so the top should stay simple. A fitted knit, a basic shirt, or a plain vest are all safe choices. For added dimension, work tonal light-and-dark variations within one colour family for harmony without monotony.

### Length and Proportion

- **Knee length**: the most versatile, suited to commuting and formal occasions.
- **Midi**: elegant and refined, a frequent summer pick.
- **Ankle length**: the most flowing in motion, ideal for holidays and evenings.

Mind the waistline when styling — tucking the top into the waistband elongates the lower body.

### Care Tips

- Keep pressed pieces away from high-heat ironing and heavy pressure that can destroy the crease.
- Store by hanging vertically or loosely rolling to reduce unwanted creasing.
- Check the wash label first; synthetics are usually more washable and better at holding pleats.
