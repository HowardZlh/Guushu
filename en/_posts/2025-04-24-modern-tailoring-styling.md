## From "Power" to "Ease"

Last century's power dressing used wide shoulders and stiff tailoring to assert authority; today's tailored suit is more relaxed — it keeps the structure and clean lines but sheds the tension, translating "power" into an easy kind of confidence.

### Three Everyday Ways to Wear It

**Commute · Sharp**
- Fitted blazer + straight-leg trousers + simple loafers
- A neutral matching suit is the safest bet, its clean cut reading as professional.

**Weekend · Relaxed**
- Oversized blazer + white tee + wide-leg trousers
- Soften the suit's edge with a supple base layer for an easy urban mood.

**Evening · Crisp**
- Blazer + silk top + heels
- A single well-cut jacket instantly elevates a simple look.

![Modern tailored look](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1539109136881-3be0616acf4b?w=800)

### The Cut Is Everything

Almost all of the tailored suit's elegance lives in the cut:

- **Shoulder line**: the right shoulder is the soul of the fit — too wide reads as bulky, too narrow loses structure.
- **Waistline**: a slight nip balances the jacket's firmness while keeping a feminine line.
- **Length**: the proportion of jacket to trouser directly decides how sharp the whole look feels.

### The Appeal of Gender-Neutral Cuts

Contemporary suiting increasingly favours neutrality and comfort — no longer relying on exaggerated shoulder pads, but shaping the silhouette through the pattern itself. This "de-gendered" cut actually reads as more modern and more versatile: it can be sharp or relaxed, depending entirely on how you style it.

> "Real power is dressing comfortably — and with conviction." —— Guushu Design Team

### Colour and Accessories

Following the wisdom of classic power dressing, neutrals like navy, grey, and black remain the safest choices. For a touch of personality, reach for a silk scarf, a brooch, or a structured handbag — accent with detail rather than shout with colour.
